Setting the stage

Our CMO, Lindsey Christensen, identified the website as a key opportunity to improve lead generation for our sales team.

We recognized several core issues:

  • The site lacked a consistent voice and tone
  • The UX and visual design were inconsistent
  • There was no long-term content strategy
  • It lacked a CMS, making even simple updates difficult for the Marketing team

Lindsey and Elaina Natario, a designer from our Boston studio, began documenting internal and external pain points. I supported the process by offering feedback, historical context, and guidance throughout.

We used this early audit to define clear Jobs-to-be-Done across three audiences: our marketing team, prospective clients, and internal contributors.

Understanding users and their journey

To complement the internal research, I conducted Jobs-to-be-Done–style interviews with clients to uncover their motivations and decision-making timeline. These interviews helped validate our assumptions and revealed how and when the site influenced their buying journey.

Establishing brand guidelines

Once we had alignment on problems and outcomes, we realized the site redesign needed to start with a stronger foundation: a consistent brand system.

Lloyd Bennett and Miles Johnson, designers from our London studio, led the creation of a refined visual identity. I partnered with Lindsey to give strategic feedback and approve deliverables. Their work became the cornerstone of every design decision that followed.

Reworking the content strategy

While the brand work progressed, Lindsey and I took on a complete audit of the website content. Much of it was company-centric, not user-focused, and failed to speak to client goals or outcomes.

We rewrote each core page—starting with Services—to better reflect the challenges our audience faced and clearly communicate how we could help.

Designing a flexible component system

As content patterns emerged, I began designing a component system—or “slices”—that could support a variety of storytelling needs across the site.

I collaborated closely with Tyson Gach, our NYC-based Design Director, to define the underlying data model and evolve the visual design. Tyson also led a massive code refactor, giving us a clean base to build and ship from.

Building with Prismic

To move quickly, we focused first on structure—implementing content and layout components in Prismic. This allowed us to validate the design system and ensure it worked across all key pages.

In tandem, we aligned our content audit to the new components, making it easy for the Marketing team to create or update pages without needing design or dev support.

Tyson and I also created documentation for the slice system to guide content creation and ensure consistency going forward.

Results

The project was a win across the board:

  • Bounce rate dropped by 23.5%
  • Conversion rate increased by 29%
  • Content was rewritten to speak with our audience, not at them
  • The majority of the site moved into a CMS, enabling the Marketing team to own and scale content independently

The project culminated in a workshop we led at thoughtbot titled “There’s a typo on the homepage!”—a lighthearted look at the power of collaboration, process, and systems thinking.
